DEBRIEF SHEET

Thank you for participating in this study!

What is this study about?

In this study, people heard a children's story and listened for particular sounds. There are two versions of this study: one that asked people to listen for short beeps and another that asked people to listen for the letter 'P' sound.

We are interested in whether people are faster at detecting sounds from a language (P's) relative to detecting non-linguistic sounds like beeps. When listening to someone speak, we can occasionally read their mind and guess the next word that they are going to say.

For example, imagine hearing "I like my coffee with cream and..."

Most likely, you were able to guess that the last word of the sentence is sugar. This ability to predict upcoming words may make people better at detecting certain sounds because they expect to hear them in the story!

Why should we do this study?

We hope this study will provide information about how people attend to sounds in their environment and to stories. Most language research involves listening to individual sentences that do not form a larger context.

We believe that listeners are better at anticipating what someone will say when they are listening to an interesting story. This type of "linguistic mind-reading" is what we are primarily interested in understanding.
